Anchorage Herald Archives


The Anchorage Herald archives hold historically important newspapers, clippings, and legal documents. The archive contains over 2,000 linear feet of material, spanning over a century of journalism.

The goals and interests of the Anchorage Herald Archives are threefold. Going in order of importance, the primary institutional priority is the preservation of all digital and physical materials, pertaining to both the publication of the newspaper and its ongoing operation. The second goal is accessibility, both internal and external. Ideally, the archive will allow for equal access to the general public for research or publication, and to Herald staff members, allowing them to find paperwork or legal documents in an efficient manner. The final objective, and lowest priority is the diversification of the collection. The archive should seek out and, when possible, accommodate for the acquisition of materials that complement the existing collection. Possible examples include materials involving Alaska, other newspapers, or Herald subscribers.
